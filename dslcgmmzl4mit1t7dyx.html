Work/Life Balance

I am finding it more and more difficult these days to have a solid work/life balance. I love my children so very much. More than I can describe, really. But I also love my job. I love learning new things and working hard and feeling a sense of accomplishment. The struggle comes with working an hour away from home. I can't take a break for dinner and run home to cuddle and kiss goodnight and then get back to it. So instead, half the time I work late enough that I don't get to see them before they're asleep for the night, and the other half I leave work at a decent time and get, but feel like I have to jump on my computer to finish some things.

I need to find a better way to balance everything. Like, stay late at work two or three nights a week, and the other nights go home at a decent time and not do any work (at least until the kids are sound asleep). It is so much easier said than done. As my friends say, and they aren't wrong, I'm a bit of a workaholic. Guess that's what happens when you love what you're doing and believe in it so much.

Veloz Viewpoints 9/20

Greetings from Kentucky. I hope you are all having a great week!

Im writing this column from my parents couch. Yes, Im in Kentucky again,

but just for the weekend. In fact, by the time you read this, Ill be back

in Nevada. But even though this visit was short, it was a great one.

Before I get too far into my visit this weekend, I want to share a story

about Analise from this past week. Analise has been trying to get her back

handspring for some time now. At some points it has been a battle of wills

to get her to even try. She was afraid to throw it without a spot. I dont

blame her, it must be scary. It isnt something I can do, so I try not to

be too hard on her, but when she doesnt even try it infuriates me. Not

being able to is one thing, not trying is another.

On Tuesday night, when I arrived at the cheer studio for a parent meeting

the coach called me into the practice. Analise was refusing to try. I

watched her try for a few minutes before the meeting was starting and I

had to step out. After the meeting I went to watch her again and she was

still not really trying and I was growing increasingly frustrated. I sent

her to the car while I made a payment. In truth, I was pretty angry with

her attitude and I needed a minute so I wouldnt yell at her. I needed

several minutes.

She came back inside before I finished making the payment and she said she

was ready to try again. And then it happened. After tears and more trying,

she finally got it! And then, she just kept doing it, over and over. In

fact, her coach got up away from her so we could all stand back and watch,

and she kept doing it. I am SO PROUD of her! I know how scared she was and

she conquered that fear and finally got her back handspring. And then on

Friday night she got her round off back handspring with a little spot. Im

just so very proud of her.

AV

I traveled to Kentucky on Thursday this past week. This time it was just

me, and it was a short visit. I arrived after a long day of traveling on

Thursday. Mom, Dad and Carol picked me up at the airport in Charleston and

we grabbed some dinner before driving home. After a little visiting, and I

had to do a little work, we all went to bed.

Friday I slept in a little before Carol climbed in the bed to chat with me.

It is always nice to wake up and chat with your sister first thing in the

morning. After that, I did some work before Mom, Carol and I went out for

lunch together. It was a great lunch with great company.

We came back from lunch and I had more work to do. Unfortunately, my job

doesnt just stop when Im not there. So I worked, and then we ate dinner

and then I worked a little more. Saturday morning we got up to go to Evans

baseball double-header. I was so very glad to watch him play as he did so

amazing! He had two solid hits that were really close to going over the

fence. I would have LOVED to witness his first out of the park homerun, but

it just wasnt to be this time. But I have no doubt hell get it soon!

We came home from the game and Mom made me chili dogs for dinner. So

yummy!! My mom is such a great cook and I love when I get to be spoiled

with it. As I write this, we have finished church and a nice lunch as a

family and we are heading to the movies soon. I love this time with my

family, but it is just never long enough. Of course, even if I were here

for several weeks, it wouldnt be enough. However, I will take what I get

and enjoy it.

Until next week, make it a great one!

Amy

Veloz Viewpoints 3/8/15

Greetings from Nevada. I hope you are all having a great week!

Man, Im tired. What a day!! This weekend, Ben participated in another Ragnar. In case you dont remember what that is, it is when teams of 12 crazy people run three-legs each to complete a total of 200 miles in approximately 36 hours. Oh, and they pay to do this! Did I mention theyre a little crazy?

Anyway, he started the race at 5 a.m. on Friday morning. They started at Mount Charleston, where the temperature was approximately 15 degrees. He ran his second leg about 12 hours later, and then ran his third leg sometime super early on Saturday morning. I dont know what time he started, but I know when he called me at 5 a.m. to make sure I was awake, he was running.

Why was I getting up at 5 a.m. on a Saturday? Because Antonio had his playoff game in Vegas and we had to be at the field, on the other end of town, at 7:30 a.m., which means we had to leave the house around 6 a.m.

I was able to get myself awake and looking less zombie-like, as well as get all the kids up and out the door on time. I honestly couldnt believe it!

We made a bathroom stop in Vegas before we got to the field, and still managed to make it to the field on time. I was proud of myself!

Antonios playoff game began at 9 a.m. Our team did the kickoff and their team was receiving. Their team bobbled the ball and our team was able to recover it, around the 20-yard line! The first drive, our team was able to score a touchdown!! We also ran in the extra point.

There was no additional scoring through the entire first half. After halftime, their team scored, but failed to run in the extra point. They almost scored again, but thankfully there was some holding in the back against them, and it was brought back. We were able to hold them and keep them from scoring again. There was no additional scoring for the rest of the game, so we won 7-6! His next playoff game is next weekendat the same place and same time. Im tired just thinking about it!

After the game we played at the park with some friends who had come to watch Antonios game. After our park play, we headed to their house so the kids could play together. Jenn was nice enough to feed us all lunch and the kids played for a while until we had to leave to go watch Blakes soccer game. We headed to the other side of Vegas, in the opposite direction, for the game. We watched her play and then the kids played in the park again for a while.

When all the parents were just DONE, we headed out to head back home. We made a bathroom stop and then swung through Sonic for some dinner before leaving Las Vegas to head back home. We arrived home at 6 p.m. Now, Im worn out.

I plan to spend my Sunday morning sleeping in!! And then hope to enjoy the day relaxing between cleaning different things in my house. There is just never enough time to do the fun things plus the cleaning things. And quite honestly, Id rather do the fun things.

Maybe Santa will bring me a maid for Christmas!

Until next week, make it a great one!
